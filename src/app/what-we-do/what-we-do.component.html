Creating safe spaces, opportunity, and dignity for street-connected children

home Aangan Day Care Centre

The daycare centre – Aangan – was started in 2008. Since then, we have been working directly with the most vulnerable street children through this centre. Streets are not a safe place for children, and Aangan provides an option for parents where they can send their children without worry.

When children are on the streets, they are prone to various forms of exploitation, harassment, and addiction. Aangan Daycare Centre at Daryaganj remains open from 10 AM to 6 PM, Monday to Saturday.

groups Praangan – Community Outreach

Praangan is Jamghat’s outreach programme engaging children and families in their street and slum communities. Conducted six days a week, it offers remedial classes, theatre, visual arts, counselling, and regular parent interactions.

Currently, the Praangan model is being run in five locations in Delhi: Shastri Park, Mithapul, Takiya Kale Khan, Kalyanpuri, and Aya Nagar. Approximately 300 children (aged 6–18 years) participate daily.

Key Goals:

  • Reintroducing children to education
  • Bridge courses for school dropouts
  • Preventing school dropouts
  • Vocational and extracurricular exposure

theater_comedy Theatre & Night Walk

We organize stage and street plays on various socio-economic issues and conduct theatre workshops. Jamghat’s director and theatre artist, Amit Kumar Sinha, conducts these workshops for participants including schools, colleges, NGOs, and civil society.

We also conduct Night Walks for the general public, students, and enthusiasts to expose them to the real situation of homelessness and generate support.

Theatre as a Tool for Awareness

Theatre has been Jamghat’s core tool of change since 2003. We have conducted over 1,500 performances, reaching and sensitizing more than 200,000 people on child rights, street situations, and community needs.
