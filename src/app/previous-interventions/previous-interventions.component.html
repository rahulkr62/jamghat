Initiatives that created long-term impact and sustainable change

home Children’s Homes – AMAN & AANCHAL

AMAN was a residential home for 16 boys and AANCHAL for 13 girls. These homes provided a safe, family-like space where children and youth could live and attend school, supported by Jamghat’s staff and volunteers.

The care model included provision of basic necessities such as food, shelter, and healthcare, with priority on education, capacity building, recreation, and adult support and guidance.

More than 100 children have passed out from these two homes and are now doing well in their lives, contributing to society as responsible citizens.

Jamghat decided to discontinue running these two CCIs. Aman Home was closed on 31st July, and Aanchal Home is scheduled to be closed by 31st March 2025.

eco Ekjut – Livelihood for Women with Environmental Sustainability

Jamghat’s 15 years of experience showed that livelihood opportunities for women can pave the way for a better future for themselves and their families, thereby addressing the economic issues that often push children to run away from their homes.

In partnership with its long-term donor and partner iPartner India, Jamghat developed a model to tackle both environmental degradation and poor living conditions of street women.

Ekjut was a tailoring unit at Jama Masjid aimed at addressing the problem of plastic use by training street women to make cotton cloth bags. These bags were then sold to a cloth manufacturing company under a 100% buy-back scheme.

These carry bags were made by women who might otherwise be begging on the streets or involved in exploitative sex work. Every bag sold not only reduced plastic usage but also provided a woman with wages and dignity.
